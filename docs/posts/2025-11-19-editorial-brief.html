The Bitcoin Miner's Report

A Shifting Landscape

Key insights from November 19, 2025

The Great Pivot: From Mining to AI & HPC

The most significant trend is the strategic pivot by major mining veterans. Faced with declining profitability, established players are leveraging their infrastructure and capital to chase the boom in AI and High-Performance Computing (HPC). This isn't just diversification; for some, it's a full exit.

Bitfury's $1B Exit

$1B

After 14 years, Bitfury is exiting Bitcoin mining to launch a $1 Billion investment fund targeting AI, Quantum Computing, and decentralized tech.

Profitability Headwinds

The "why" behind the pivot is clear. Mining difficulty has surged 52% year-over-year, while the Bitcoin price has fallen 26.2% from its recent October peak, squeezing margins for all but the most efficient operators.

The Hardware Paradox

Contradicting the profitability crunch, hardware manufacturer Canaan (CAN) posted stellar Q3 results. This suggests a "fork" in the market: while some veterans exit, well-capitalized (often North American) miners are aggressively upgrading to next-generation hardware, driving a race for absolute efficiency.

Canaan (CAN) Revenue Surge (Q3)

Canaan's revenue doubled to $150.5 million, a 104.4% year-over-year increase, smashing expectations. This was driven by high demand for new mining rigs, particularly from large-scale North American buyers.

Canaan (CAN) Stock Jump

+21%

The market reacted strongly to the news, with CAN stock surging 21% on Tuesday as investors recognized the robust demand for next-gen mining equipment.

The Regulatory Hammer: Energy Theft

Operational risk remains a critical factor, especially in jurisdictions with unclear regulations. Malaysia has become a case study, with the government launching a massive crackdown on illegal mining operations that have cost the national utility over a billion dollars.

Malaysia's Loss to Power Theft

$1.11B

(RM4.57 Billion) from 2020 to August 2025. This staggering loss from 13,827 illegal premises is forcing regulators to act decisively.

The government is responding by installing smart meters to detect suspicious energy use in real-time, aiming to curb future losses.

Wall Street's Selective Bets

Analysts are not treating all miners equally. New coverage shows strong optimism for large-scale, publicly-traded miners like Riot and IREN, suggesting a belief in their ability to scale and navigate the efficiency race. However, Cipher Mining's (CIFR) 16% drop, even after announcing $1.4B in funding for its own HPC pivot, shows market uncertainty and selectivity.

The chart shows the newly initiated analyst price target upside for Riot and IREN, contrasted with Cipher's recent one-day drop. This highlights the market's mixed, highly-selective sentiment toward the sector.

The Retail On-Ramp: Cloud Mining

While industrial giants grapple with billion-dollar pivots and regulatory hammers, the retail narrative remains focused on accessibility. Cloud mining platforms are being heavily marketed as an "easy" and "hardware-free" way for individuals to earn Bitcoin, abstracting away all the operational complexities.
